Worldly Implications

The effects of climate change are already directly affecting third world countries. Countries dependent on tourism, agriculture, forestry, and ocean resources will be hit the hardest by increasing global temperatures, more frequent natural disasters, and decreasing crop yields. These effects will only become more extreme in the coming decades if we continue to support a fossil fuel-powered world.

Over 50 percent of the world's coral reefs have died off in the last 30 years, and scientists predict that 90 percent will be gone in the next 30 years. Already, 70,000 square miles of forest has died off in the Rocky Mountains. Arctic life is struggling to survive, with the polar bear being the first animal listed as endangered due to the effects of climate change. These are only a few of the early warning signs of the damage that will be done by climate change, if we do not act soon.

Impact on You

Infrastructure is threatened by the onset of a warmer climate, and energy supplies will become more sparse as the temperature continues to rise. The farming industry is heavily impacted by unpredictable, extreme weather changes. Unprecedented heatwaves and a rising sea level can cause unpredictable changes in water supply that will burden the crop yield. A warmer atmosphere also increases smog, which causes direct damage to our lungs. It also serves to accelerate forest fires, and directly impacts our freshwater supply. Without swift action to combat climate change, our children and grandchildren will suffer the consequences.
